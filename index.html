In their own words

Turning ambiguity into systems — and systems into outcomes.

My career path has been anything but linear — and that's the point. I've moved between design, data, and operations not by accident, but because I genuinely believe the best products live at the intersection of all three.

At Microsoft, I learned that telemetry without empathy is just noise. At NC State OIT, I learned that a well-designed template can quietly unlock 50 people's capacity. And every internship, mentorship, and event I've organized has reinforced the same truth: systems only matter if the people inside them feel seen.

I'm especially drawn to teams that empower curiosity and craft — where design decisions are intentional, ambiguity is treated as a design challenge, and impact is something you can actually measure.

This website is the UX project I want to present to you. Not just a portfolio — a proof of how I think.

Let's build something meaningful.
